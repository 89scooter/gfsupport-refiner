# GoFreight Knowledge Base Writing Guideline

(Customer-first + AI-friendly)

---

## Purpose

Refine existing Knowledge Base content to improve clarity, readability, structure, and AI retrieval quality.

The output must remain a Knowledge Base article.

Do not convert the source into another content format.

---

## Output Principles

Refinement should:

* Preserve the original intent.
* Improve readability.
* Improve wording consistency.
* Improve customer understanding.
* Improve AI retrieval quality.

Do NOT:

* Rewrite from scratch.
* Reduce content depth.
* Change document purpose.
* Remove operational details.

---

## KB Writing Guardrails

Maintain Knowledge Base writing style.

Do NOT convert instructional content into:

* Blog writing
* Marketing writing
* Promotional language
* Sales messaging

Avoid unsupported wording such as:

* effective
* seamless
* powerful
* quickly
* easily
* challenge
* discover
* unlock
* transform
* optimize

Prefer neutral support wording:

* use
* navigate
* configure
* review
* generate
* export
* locate
* select
* update
* edit
* verify

Preserve instructional tone.

Do not introduce:

* value judgments
* recommendations
* product claims
* unsupported benefits
* feature assumptions

Keep explanations operational and customer-action oriented.

---

## Author Voice Preservation

Preserve the original article tone and opening intent.

Do NOT:

* rewrite introductory paragraphs unless wording is unclear
* replace purpose statements with generic AI wording
* standardize headings unnecessarily
* rewrite examples into summaries

Prefer:

minimal refinement over stylistic rewrite.

---

## Content Preservation Rules

Preserve original document intent and content depth.

Do NOT:

* Convert step-by-step instructions into summaries
* Remove navigation paths
* Collapse procedures into short answers
* Rewrite detailed explanations into generic statements
* Remove examples
* Remove warnings
* Remove notes
* Remove screenshots
* Remove operational context

When source contains:

* numbered steps
* menu navigation
* configuration fields
* report descriptions
* filtering behavior
* examples

preserve them as structured instructional content.

Maintain:

* section hierarchy
* procedural order
* list formatting
* operational details

Refinement should improve wording and readability only.

Never reduce article completeness.

---

## Procedure Preservation

If source contains procedures:

* preserve every navigation step
* preserve filter fields
* preserve examples
* preserve menu labels

Do NOT:

* merge multiple actions into one sentence
* combine steps
* simplify execution flow

Each operation must remain independently executable.

---

## FAQ Boundary

Do not add FAQ sections unless the source article already implies repeated user questions.

Avoid converting procedural guides into FAQ style.

---

## Troubleshooting Boundary

Do not introduce troubleshooting sections if the source is not troubleshooting content.

Do not create root cause analysis unless explicitly present.

---

## HTML Preservation

Preserve:

* headings
* tables
* lists
* emphasis
* screenshots
* hyperlinks

Keep HTML structure stable whenever possible.

Do not flatten layout.

---

## Refinement Philosophy

Refinement means:

Original Article
→ Improve wording
→ Improve readability
→ Preserve structure

Not:

Original Article
→ Summarize
→ Rebuild
→ Rewrite
