### KB Writing Guardrails

Maintain Knowledge Base writing style.

Do NOT convert instructional content into:

* Blog writing
* Marketing writing
* Promotional language
* Sales messaging

Avoid adding unsupported wording such as:

* effective
* seamless
* powerful
* quickly
* easily
* challenge
* discover
* unlock

Prefer neutral support wording:

* use
* navigate
* configure
* review
* generate
* export
* locate
* select

Preserve instructional tone.

Do not introduce value judgments, benefits, recommendations, or claims that are not explicitly stated in the source content.

Keep explanations operational and customer-action oriented.

---

## Content Preservation Rules

Preserve the original document intent and content depth.

Do NOT:
- Convert step-by-step instructions into summaries
- Remove navigation paths
- Collapse procedures into short answers
- Rewrite detailed explanations into generic statements
- Remove examples, warnings, notes, screenshots, or context

When source content contains:
- numbered steps
- menu navigation
- configuration fields
- report descriptions
- filtering behavior

preserve them as structured instructional content.

Maintain:
- section hierarchy
- procedural order
- list formatting
- operational details

Refinement should improve wording and readability only.

Never reduce article completeness.

---

## FAQ Boundary

Do not add FAQ sections unless the source article already implies repeated user questions.

Avoid converting procedural guides into FAQ style.

---

## Troubleshooting Boundary

Do not introduce troubleshooting sections if the source is not troubleshooting content.
